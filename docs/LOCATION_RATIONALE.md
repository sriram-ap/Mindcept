# LOCATION_RATIONALE.md

Why the Our Reach footprint is what it is (V1.3). Principle: **credible
coverage, not a padded pin count.** Only locations that strengthen
MindCept's industrial/warehousing advisory positioning are shown as
markets; sub-city nodes are shown as corridors.

## Candidate hubs shown as CORRIDORS, not new pins

The V1.3 brief listed Chakan, Sanand, Hosur, Aurangabad. These are
**sub-city industrial nodes**, not standalone markets — adding them as map
pins would clutter the map and overlap existing cities. They are
represented in the Industrial Corridors view instead:

| Node | Represented via corridor |
|---|---|
| Chakan | Mumbai–Pune Belt (Automotive – Chakan) |
| Sanand | Ahmedabad–Sanand (Automotive – Sanand) |
| Hosur | Chennai–Sri City (Automotive – Hosur belt) |
| Aurangabad | (Central/West auto belt — candidate for a future corridor) |

This satisfies "review whether additional industrial hubs deserve
inclusion" **without** randomly increasing the city count — the explicit
guard rail. Coverage depth is expressed through corridors + regional
capability, which is how a consulting firm communicates geographic reach.
